    Global Innovation Index

    The Global Innovation Index (GII) measures innovation performance across countries using a composite set of indicators covering institutions, human capital, infrastructure, market sophistication, and knowledge outputs. In this project, GII functions as the central innovation outcome variable, allowing direct comparison of national innovative capacity across countries. Its standardized methodology makes it suitable for linking innovation to income levels and to policy-relevant inputs such as research and development investment.

    Distribution of Global Innovation Index Scores Across Countries (2022)

    This distribution shows substantial cross-country variation in innovation performance. Most countries cluster in the lower to middle range of GII scores, indicating that high innovation capacity is relatively rare rather than evenly distributed. A small group of countries occupies the upper tail of the distribution, highlighting the concentration of advanced innovation systems in a limited number of economies. The overall spread and skewness of the distribution suggest that innovation gaps are structural, with clear separation between innovation leaders and the majority of countries.
